# NotebookLM Audio Overview — Setup Guide

Upload any document and get a podcast-style audio summary generated by two AI hosts.

## What It Is
Google's NotebookLM reads your sources (PDFs, articles, notes) and generates a natural-sounding two-person conversation that explains the content. It sounds like a real podcast — not text-to-speech.

## Setup (Free)

1. Go to [notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com) — requires a Google account
2. Click **+ New Notebook**
3. Add sources (up to 50 per notebook):
   - Upload PDFs, Google Docs, or paste text
   - Or paste a URL (YouTube video, website, article)
   - Click **Add**
4. Once sources are uploaded, click the **Audio Overview** button (headphone icon in the top right of the notebook)
5. Click **Generate** — takes 2–5 minutes
6. Hit play to listen — two AI hosts discuss your content as a conversation
7. Click the **⋮** menu → **Download** to save the MP3

## Best Use Cases
- Summarize a long PDF or research paper into a 10-min listen
- Turn your own notes into an audio review session
- Create explainer audio for complex topics (great for content repurposing)

## Tips
- More specific sources = better audio quality. One focused PDF beats 10 random URLs.
- The hosts sometimes get things wrong — treat it as a summary, not a source of truth
- You can't control what the hosts say — the output is fully auto-generated
- 50 sources per notebook is the current free limit

## What It Can't Do
- You cannot give the hosts a script or direct them
- No custom voices or host selection
- Download is limited to one audio file per notebook (regenerate to get a new version)
